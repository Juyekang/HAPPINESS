Happiness is used in the context of mental or emotional states, including positive or pleasant emotions ranging from contentment to intense joy.
[1] It is also used in the context of life satisfaction, such as subjective well-being, eudaimonia, flourishing and well-being.[2][3]
Since the 1960s, happiness research has been conducted in a wide variety of scientific disciplines, including gerontology, social psychology,
clinical and medical research and happiness economics.
